About

printf("a lifelong hobby");

I've been programming since my early teens. My first taster came when we got a PlayStation 2, oddly enough. It came bundled with a demo disc, and one of the things on it was Yabasic, a simple BASIC interpreter.

Interestingly enough, these demo discs shipped only in the PAL region between 2000 and 2003, and included Yabasic as an attempt to classify the PS2 as a personal computer instead of a video game console, for tax reasons. Thanks to CTurt for this info, found in his article here.

I had a play with it, mostly just editing code that was included as templates, or typing in Yabasic code from PS2 magazines. I was hooked.

As chance would have it, one of my uncles was also into software development, and after I showed him Yabasic he gave me a copy of a Visual Basic 6 book along with a copy of Microsoft Visual Basic 6 and MSDN (both CDs). I've been learning ever since.

printf("and always a hobby");

For various reasons I've never really used any of this knowledge for work. It has come in handy, along with my general computer knowledge, but I've never been employed as a developer of any kind.

I'm hoping that will change soon.
